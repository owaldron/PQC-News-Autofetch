# NIST PQC Signatures — 2026-07-21

Initial backfill of the most essential articles covering NIST's additional
post-quantum digital signature standardization process ("on-ramp"), from the
2022 call through the May 2026 Round 3 announcement and recent cryptanalysis.

---

### [10/10] NIST Advances 9 Candidates to the 3rd Round of Additional PQC Digital Signatures
- **Date:** 2026-05-14
- **Authors:** NIST CSRC
- **Summary:** NIST selected 9 of 14 Round 2 candidates for Round 3: FAEST, HAWK, MAYO, MQOM, QR-UOV, SDitH, SNOVA, SQIsign, and UOV. Five code-based MPCitH schemes (CROSS, LESS, Mirath, PERK, RYDE) were eliminated. Round 3 specification tweaks are due August 14, 2026; the evaluation is expected to last ~2 years, with the 7th PQC Standardization Conference planned for spring/summer 2027.
- **Link:** https://csrc.nist.gov/News/2026/nist-advances-9-candidates-to-the-3rd-round-of-pqc

### [10/10] NIST IR 8610 — Status Report on the Second Round of Additional Digital Signature Schemes
- **Date:** 2026-05-14
- **Authors:** NIST (Gorjan Alagic, Daniel Apon, Dustin Moody, et al.)
- **Summary:** Official status report documenting the 18-month second-round evaluation, including public cryptanalysis, performance benchmarking, and results from the Sixth PQC Standardization Conference (September 2025). Provides detailed technical assessments and rationale for advancing the 9 schemes. Among MPCitH candidates, NIST expressed highest confidence in FAEST's security, selected MQOM for computational performance, and SDitH for diversity via the Syndrome Decoding Problem.
- **Link:** https://csrc.nist.gov/pubs/ir/8610/final

### [9/10] NIST IR 8528 — Status Report on the First Round + Round 2 Announcement
- **Date:** 2024-10-24
- **Authors:** NIST
- **Summary:** Official first-round status report documenting evaluation of 40 initial submissions and selection of 14 for Round 2. The 14 candidates span code-based (2), isogeny-based (1: SQIsign), lattice-based (1: HAWK), MPC-in-the-Head (5), multivariate (4), and symmetric (1) categories.
- **Link:** https://csrc.nist.gov/pubs/ir/8528/final

### [8/10] Why We Cannot Wait for Better Post-Quantum Signature Algorithms
- **Date:** 2026-07-09
- **Authors:** Bas Westerbaan (Cloudflare)
- **Summary:** A practitioner's analysis arguing that none of the on-ramp candidates will be standardized before ~2030 (SQIsign likely not before 2035), so ML-DSA must be deployed now despite its larger sizes. Provides detailed assessments of SQIsign's complexity/performance issues and HAWK's "headwinds" from LIP cryptanalysis, including discussion of the Lars Ran "Wedges" attack on UOV and the June 2026 HAWK attack papers.
- **Link:** https://blog.cloudflare.com/ml-dsa-will-have-to-do/

### [8/10] Exploiting SNOVA's Structure in the Wedge Product Attack
- **Date:** 2026-02 (ePrint); presented at 6th PQC Conference, September 2025
- **Authors:** Maxime Bros, Hung Le, Thibauld Lichtinger, Brice Minaud, Ray Perlner, Daniel Smith-Tone, Gerson Valenzuela
- **Summary:** Extends wedge product attacks against SNOVA, breaking 5 of the 10 current SNOVA parameter sets submitted to Round 2. One parameter set sees its security drop from 310 bits to 189 bits. Contributed to NIST's requirement that SNOVA update parameters by August 2026.
- **Link:** https://eprint.iacr.org/2026/237

### [8/10] Improved Cryptanalysis of SNOVA
- **Date:** 2024-08 (ePrint); published at Eurocrypt 2025
- **Authors:** Ward Beullens
- **Summary:** Demonstrates that SNOVA implicitly uses a structured version of the "whipping" technique from MAYO, making it vulnerable to new forgery attacks that reduce security margins by a factor between 2^8 and 2^39. When parameter l = 2 or 3, SNOVA exhibits weak keys; l = 4 still meets NIST security levels I and III.
- **Link:** https://eprint.iacr.org/2024/1297

### [8/10] Cryptanalysis of HAWK: a Guessing Game
- **Date:** 2026-06-25 (revised 2026-07-05)
- **Authors:** Ben Nelson, Joshua Limbrey, Cong Ling, Andrew Mendelsohn (Imperial College London)
- **Summary:** Claims a classical algorithm recovering the HAWK secret key in probabilistic polynomial time, assuming four number-theoretic heuristics. The authors explicitly do NOT claim HAWK is broken — Heuristic 4 was found insufficient (the algorithm appears super-polynomial due to a miscounting error). The most aggressive cryptanalytic result against HAWK to date.
- **Link:** https://eprint.iacr.org/2026/1318

### [7/10] HAWK "Guessing Game" is not Polynomial-Time
- **Date:** 2026 (ePrint)
- **Authors:** Markku-Juhani O. Saarinen
- **Summary:** Direct response to the "Guessing Game" paper, arguing the claimed polynomial-time attack on HAWK is not actually polynomial-time. This back-and-forth demonstrates active cryptanalytic scrutiny of HAWK's Module-LIP security assumption during the NIST evaluation.
- **Link:** https://eprint.iacr.org/2026/1377.pdf

### [7/10] Wedges, Oil, and Vinegar — An Analysis of UOV in the Exterior Algebra
- **Date:** 2025-06-18
- **Authors:** Lars Ran
- **Summary:** Proposes a new key-recovery attack on UOV over fields of characteristic 2 using polar forms as elements of the exterior algebra. Recovers the secret oil space via sparse linear algebra, reducing security of UOV-Ip, UOV-III, and UOV-V by 4, 11, and 20 bits respectively, and MAYO2's security by 28 bits.
- **Link:** https://eprint.iacr.org/2025/1143

### [7/10] SUF-CMA SQISign via Canonical Response Encoding
- **Date:** 2026-06
- **Authors:** Dustin Ray
- **Summary:** Identifies a concrete malleability vulnerability in SQIsign v2.0: the basis change matrix in signatures can be negated modulo 2^N to produce a distinct valid signature for the same message (analogous to ECDSA's (r,s)/(r,n-s) issue). Proposes a fix to upgrade SQIsign from EUF-CMA to SUF-CMA security — significant because all prior proofs only established EUF-CMA.
- **Link:** https://eprint.iacr.org/2026/1305

### [7/10] Cryptanalysis of Definite and Indefinite Lattice Isomorphism Problems With Applications to HAWK and DEFI
- **Date:** 2026-06-17
- **Authors:** Markus Kirschmer, Cong Ling, Ali Sadreddin
- **Summary:** Studies LIP for definite and indefinite quadratic forms. Successfully breaks DEFIv2 (recovering secret keys within minutes), but explicitly states the techniques do NOT compromise HAWK's security. The structural properties exploited against DEFI do not apply to HAWK's construction.
- **Link:** https://eprint.iacr.org/2026/890

### [7/10] A Look at the Latest Post-Quantum Signature Standardization Candidates
- **Date:** 2024
- **Authors:** Cloudflare
- **Summary:** Deep technical comparison of the on-ramp candidates, evaluating signature sizes, key sizes, and performance. Discusses MAYO's compact signatures, HAWK's fast signing without floating-point arithmetic, and SQIsign's remarkably small signatures (148 bytes) but slow verification. Valuable as a practitioner-oriented overview.
- **Link:** https://blog.cloudflare.com/another-look-at-pq-signatures/

### [6/10] New Security Analysis for UOV-based Signature Candidates (Fifth PQC Standardization Conference)
- **Date:** 2024
- **Authors:** NIST conference paper
- **Summary:** Conference paper evaluating the impact of MinRank and intersection attacks on UOV, MAYO, and QR-UOV parameter sets. Provides updated security analysis relevant to the multivariate candidates advancing through the standardization process.
- **Link:** https://csrc.nist.gov/csrc/media/Events/2024/fifth-pqc-standardization-conference/documents/papers/new-security-analysis-for-uov.pdf

### [6/10] Hash Your Keys before Signing: BUFF Security of the Additional NIST PQC Signatures
- **Date:** 2024
- **Authors:** Academic researchers (ePrint 2024/591)
- **Summary:** Analyzes Beyond UnForgeability Features (BUFF) security properties of the additional NIST PQC signature candidates. Found that only three schemes — CROSS, HAWK, and PROV — achieve BUFF security out of the box, while others may need modifications. Important for understanding which candidates offer stronger security guarantees.
- **Link:** https://eprint.iacr.org/2024/591
